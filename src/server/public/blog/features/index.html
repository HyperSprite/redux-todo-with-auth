Welcome to A Race athlete

It’s time to level up and get ready for your A race!

araceathlete.com is a place to share and track events, keep an eye on your Watts per Kilograms and see how your week is progressing at a glance.

What is an A race you ask? While you may have other important events, an A race is THE event you plan and train for all year. It is the pinnacle of your season. It is the point of all that suffering.

But we digress, by connecting with your Strava account, you can start tracking your fitness in preparation for your A Race and get insights into how your week is stacking up. Put away those spreadsheets and let us do the tracking.

How do I get started?

Getting started is easy. All you need to do is click the Connect with STRAVA button in the upper right corner. You will be redirected to Strava to sign in and they will return you, logged in and ready to go.

Once inside, use the icon in the upper left to open the Menu Drawer.

Advanced features, such as adding new events, also requires membership to the A Race athlete Strava club (here).

Power at Altitude

Power at Altitude is an estimation of your FTP at various altitudes so you can better regulate your power expenditure away from home. The chart is based on your City and Country in Strava (here) and current FTP (here) using the Bassett (1) formulas. Because FTP is a Premium Strava feature, the Power at Altitude customized chart is only available to Strava Premium users at this time. This may change in the future.

How can I use this? The measure of a Watt does not change with altitude, so it is easy to redline yourself because you try to match your normal power or become disheartened because your legs do not meet your expectations. Either way, it will set you up for a harder race than needed. What if the number you train to is in sync with the altitude?

Most cycling computers now have Power/FTP. Set up your primary display to show Power/FTP instead of Watts and train using this. Power Zones typically look like this:

Your FTP should be right in the middle of Zone 4. This is why it is important to assess your FTP.

Now when you change altitude, check the Power at Altitude chart and adjust your head unit FTP accordingly and Power/FTP now takes altitude into account. Now you can go out and ride to the same numbers you do at home without the headgames.

Weekly Stats

What does this do?

Weekly stats are based on metrics collected during a week’s worth of Strava activities. This gives the user a quick way to see how their week is shaping up, as well as compare different metrics to each other.

Not all metrics are available to all users. A Strava Premium account is required for advanced analytics such as Power and Heart Rate. To find out more about that, visit Strava here. A Power Meter and Heart Rate sensor is also required for their respective data.

LOAD ANOTHER WEEK button

Click this button to get another previous week of stats.

CHECK STRAVA FOR NEW ACTIVITIES button

Goes out to Strava and downloads new Activities and updates the stats.

Activities

Click the expander in the upper right hand corner of the Week to see the Activities the Week’s Stats are based on. These show the same metrics and have a Delete option. The Delete only deletes the activity from A Race athlete It does not delete it from Strava. If you rename or use the elevation correction feature on Strava, you will need to delete the activity on A Race athlete so it can be re-downloaded to reflect those changes.

Units of measure are based on your Strava Display Preferences (here).

Basic Metrics

Distance, Time and Elevation are available to all Strava users.

TSS and Kilojoules - Power Meter

TSS, short for Training Stress Score, also know as Training Load in a Strava Activity or Training Impulse on the Strava Fitness and Freshness chart (here), is a number based on power produced and your current FTP. That looks a bit like this: ((Elapsed Time * Weighted Average Watts * (Weighted Average Watts / FTP)) / FTP * 3600)

FTP: A Race athlete uses your Strava FTP (here). Keep this up to date for accurate statistics. Originally FTP was based on the amount of power an athlete could produce over an hour. Strava provides an estimate on the Strava Power Curve chart (here ). If you have a few sufficiently long, hard outdoor rides, this number is usually close enough as a starting point. For More on FTP see: TrainingPeaks: What Is Functional Threshold Power? Global Cycling Network (video): How To Do A Functional Threshold Power (FTP) Test TrainerRoad: 20 Minute VS. 8 Minute FTP Test

Kilojoules is the power produced over the whole of the activity. It is closely linked with actual Calories expended.

Suffer Score and Calories - Heart Rate

Strava uses your Maximum Heart Rate and your ride data to determine a Suffer Score. You can set your Maximum on Strava here. It is dependent on your Maximum being correct and you being in good health. Calories are based on your heart rate over the length of the activity and are not as precise as Kilojoules for measuring energy expenditure. That said, if you don’t have a power meter, Heart Rate is the next best thing.

How can I make use of this?

One way to make use of this weekly data is to set weekly TSS or Suffer Score goals. Mind you, not all TSS or Suffering is created equal, but it can help if you are mixing indoor interval sessions and outdoor rides.

Power trumps Heart Rate, so use TSS if available.

Events

Events are a list of community-curated endurance events. This list is all about the future. For reference purposes, past events will be featured on another page in an upcoming release.

Click on the stars next to the titles to “Favorite” events you plan to do and then filter them using the All, Star, or Owner buttons in the top right.

Below the title are the days or weeks until the event takes place, making it easy to schedule your season and training.

Don’t see an event on here you want to do? Just press the Add button in the lower right and add your own event.

If you don’t see the add button, you probably forgot to join the A Race athlete club on Strava (here).

At this time, Strava lets you enter your current FTP (here) and Weight (here), but it only keeps this as a single value and does not save a history. Update your Strava numbers as necessary and A Race athlete will keep track of this for you on this page. We will also calculate and track your Watts per Kilogram while we are at it.
