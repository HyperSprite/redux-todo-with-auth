Weekly Stats

What does this do?

Weekly stats are based on metrics collected during a week’s worth of Strava activities. This gives the user a quick way to see how their week is shaping up, as well as compare different metrics to each other.

Not all metrics are available to all users. A Strava Premium account is required for advanced analytics such as Power and Heart Rate. To find out more about that, visit Strava here. A Power Meter and Heart Rate sensor is also required for their respective data.

LOAD ANOTHER WEEK button

Click this button to get another previous week of stats.

CHECK STRAVA FOR NEW ACTIVITIES button

Goes out to Strava and downloads new Activities and updates the stats.

Activities

Click the expander in the upper right hand corner of the Week to see the Activities the Week’s Stats are based on. These show the same metrics and have a Delete option. The Delete only deletes the activity from A Race athlete It does not delete it from Strava. If you rename or use the elevation correction feature on Strava, you will need to delete the activity on A Race athlete so it can be re-downloaded to reflect those changes.

Units of measure are based on your Strava Display Preferences (here).

What does this mean?

Basic Metrics

Distance, Time and Elevation are available to all Strava users.

TSS and Kilojoules - Power Meter

TSS, short for Training Stress Score, also know as Training Load in a Strava Activity or Training Impulse on the Strava Fitness and Freshness chart (here), is a number based on power produced and your current FTP. That looks a bit like this: ((Elapsed Time * Weighted Average Watts * (Weighted Average Watts / FTP)) / FTP * 3600)

FTP: A Race athlete uses your Strava FTP (here). Keep this up to date for accurate statistics. Originally FTP was based on the amount of power an athlete could produce over an hour. Strava provides an estimate on the Strava Power Curve chart (here ). If you have a few sufficiently long, hard outdoor rides, this number is usually close enough as a starting point. For More on FTP see: TrainingPeaks: What Is Functional Threshold Power? Global Cycling Network (video): How To Do A Functional Threshold Power (FTP) Test TrainerRoad: 20 Minute VS. 8 Minute FTP Test

Kilojoules is the power produced over the whole of the activity. It is closely linked with actual Calories expended.

Suffer Score and Calories - Heart Rate

Strava uses your Maximum Heart Rate and your ride data to determine a Suffer Score. You can set your Maximum on Strava here. It is dependent on your Maximum being correct and you being in good health. Calories are based on your heart rate over the length of the activity and are not as precise as Kilojoules for measuring energy expenditure. That said, if you don’t have a power meter, Heart Rate is the next best thing.

How can I make use of this?

One way to make use of this weekly data is to set weekly TSS or Suffer Score goals. Mind you, not all TSS or Suffering is created equal, but it can help if you are mixing indoor interval sessions and outdoor rides.

Power trumps Heart Rate, so use TSS if available.
