Power at Altitude is an estimation of your FTP at various altitudes so you can better regulate your power expenditure away from home. The chart is based on your City and Country in Strava (here) and current FTP (here) using the Bassett (1) formulas. Because FTP is a Premium Strava feature, the Power at Altitude customized chart is only available to Strava Premium users at this time. This may change in the future.

How can I use this? The measure of a Watt does not change with altitude, so it is easy to redline yourself because you try to match your normal power or become disheartened because your legs do not meet your expectations. Either way, it will set you up for a harder race than needed. What if the number you train to is in sync with the altitude?

Most cycling computers now have Power/FTP. Set up your primary display to show Power/FTP instead of Watts and train using this. Power Zones typically look like this:

*This table is available by choosing an FTP in the Power at Altitude chart.*

Your FTP should be right in the middle of Zone 4. This is why it is important to assess your FTP.

Now when you change altitude, check the Power at Altitude chart and adjust your head unit FTP accordingly and Power/FTP now takes altitude into account. Now you can go out and ride to the same numbers you do at home without the headgames.

The measurement preference for Meters or Feet is based on Strava (here). To convert it in real time, just click the M / F toggle.
